What you’d be hiring — and what we cover instead

We’ve looked at hundreds of these job ads.
Here’s the pattern.

“Office Administrator”

Quote follow-ups in spreadsheets. Inbox triage. Booking calendars. Chasing supplier orders. Onboarding new staff. Filing forms.

We connect your inbox, CRM, and supplier portals once, then automate the chase. Forms become workflows. Onboarding becomes a checklist that runs itself. Humans are in the loop only where they need to be.

“IT Support Officer”

Resetting passwords. Setting up laptops. M365 license confusion. VPN broken again. Printer mysteries. Backup nobody’s checked in six months.

Helpdesk, device management, M365 admin, network monitoring, and backups — all included in the fixed monthly. We pick up the phone when something breaks. We see the alert before you do.

“Operations / Office Coordinator”

Compliance deadlines you almost missed. Insurance renewals. Scheduling tradies. Supplier price comparisons. End-of-month reconciliations the bookkeeper keeps asking about.

We build the reminders that actually reach you, the dashboards that compare suppliers automatically, and the integrations that stop your bookkeeper’s monthly anxiety. The repetitive decisions become rules.

When you should still hire

We’ll tell you when this doesn’t fit.

Some roles are genuinely person-shaped, not process-shaped. If your next hire is a salesperson who builds relationships, a tradesperson who needs to be on-site, a clinician who delivers care, or a leader who shapes the team — hire them. We’ll tell you that on the first call.

Where we shine is the role you keep needing to hire because the work is steady, repetitive, and software-shaped. The 20% of human time that’s real judgment, we leave with you. The 80% that’s clicks, copy-paste, and reminders, we take.
